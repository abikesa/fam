This is the realm of nihilism and deconstruction, where meaning is latent and malleable, awaiting reconstruction. Here, perspective and attention are crucial, as they determine how raw textual input is processed and integrated into coherent thought. The mind navigates this space like a sculptor chiseling away at a block of marble, seeking to uncover the latent meaning within. This process is inherently unstable, as meaning is not yet fixed, and the act of reconstruction is always contingent on the observer‚Äôs perspective. The pretext, then, is a space of potentiality, where language is both a tool and a battleground for the formation of meaning.

Moving to the subtext, language begins to take on structure and relational order. This layer functions as a lens, framing meaning and providing a scaffold for free-floating concepts. The subtext is where grammar and syntax emerge, transforming chaotic input into comprehensible patterns. It is akin to the historical layer in CG-BEST or the adaptive function in the immune model, where systems of order are imposed on complexity. This framing is not neutral; it shapes how meaning is perceived and understood. The subtext is the eye of the text, the point of focus that determines what is seen and what is obscured. It is here that language begins to acquire its relational power, forming the patterns that allow for communication and interpretation. Yet, this structure is also a constraint, as it limits the possibilities of meaning to what can be contained within its grammatical and syntactical boundaries.

At the level of text, language takes on agency, moving beyond mere existence to active engagement. This is the space of prosody and dialectics, where words interact with other words, texts, and ideas. The text is no longer passive; it acts, argues, and shapes understanding. The text, then, is not just a vehicle for meaning but an active participant in its creation.

The context is where language becomes a tool for navigating the world, a means of expressing and responding to the demands of life. It is here that words take on the rhythm of existence, moving in sync with the pulse of human experience. The context, then, is the bridge between language and life, the point where text becomes action.

Finally, the metatext transcends the boundaries of language itself, connecting text to the outside world. This is the realm of cacophony and symphony, where language either dissolves into chaos or achieves harmony. The metatext is the culmination of the preceding layers, the point where language reaches its fullest expression. # Create and visualize the neural network graph
def visualize_nn():
    layers = define_layers()
    colors = assign_colors()
    edges = define_edges()
    black_edges = define_black_edges()
    
    G = nx.DiGraph()
    pos = {}
    node_colors = []
    
    # Create mapping from original node names to numbered labels
    mapping = {}
    counter = 1
    for layer in layers.values():
        for node in layer:
            mapping[node] = f"{counter}. {node}"
            counter += 1
            
    # Add nodes with new numbered labels and assign positions
    for i, (layer_name, nodes) in enumerate(layers.items()):
        positions = calculate_positions(nodes, x_offset=i * 2)
        for node, position in zip(nodes, positions):
            new_node = mapping[node]
            G.add_node(new_node, layer=layer_name)
            pos[new_node] = position
            node_colors.append(colors.get(node, 'lightgray'))
    
    # Add edges with updated node labels
    edge_colors = []
    for (source, target), weight in edges.items():
        if source in mapping and target in mapping:
            new_source = mapping[source]
            new_target = mapping[target]
            G.add_edge(new_source, new_target, weight=weight)
            edge_colors.append('black' if (source, target) in black_edges else 'lightgrey')
    
    # Draw the graph
    plt.figure(figsize=(12, 8))
    edges_labels = {(u, v): d["weight"] for u, v, d in G.edges(data=True)}
    
    nx.draw(
        G, pos, with_labels=True, node_color=node_colors, edge_color=edge_colors,
        node_size=3000, font_size=9, connectionstyle="arc3,rad=0.2"
    )
    nx.draw_networkx_edge_labels(G, pos, edge_labels=edges_labels, font_size=8)
